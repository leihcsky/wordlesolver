Hint 3 Blue Category Clue

  • Hint 4 (purple): Last resort: read the final syllables as vehicles, not dictionary definitions.

Today’s Connections Analysis

Yellow band Fairy-tale trail set

How to identify it: Hansel-and-Gretel props and settings—forest path, trail markers, witch’s oven, and the antagonist herself.

Possible confusion: FOREST also fits nature themes; here all four must tie to that one folk tale.

Green band Cereal morsels

How to identify it: Pieces in the bowl—O-shaped loops, flakes, clustered grains, and puffed bites.

Possible confusion: LOOP tempts music or geometry; PUFF tempts pastry—test the breakfast rule on all four.

Blue band One actress’s films

How to identify it: Demi Moore starring roles—pottery romance with a spirit, legal thriller, strip-club drama, and recent horror satire.

Possible confusion: GHOST also fits yesterday’s compound puzzle; here it must be a movie title, not a prefix word.

Purple band Hidden transport endings

How to identify it: Read the tail of each long word as a vehicle—bus, car, train, ship—inside incubus, Oscar, quatrain, situationship.

Possible confusion: OSCAR tempts awards; QUATRAIN tempts poetry—test the transport suffix on all four.

How to Solve Today’s Puzzle Step by Step

  1. Submit the fairy-tale quartet first—it clears four storybook words and separates crumbs from cereal.

Hint 1 Yellow Category Clue

Hint 2 Green Category Clue

Each word can mean reasonable, valid, or well-founded in everyday speech (“that plan sounds ___”). Do not chase geometry, music volume, or compass directions unless every clue fits that sense.

Hint 3 Blue Category Clue

These are names for marks on a keyboard or in code—not kitchen plumbing alone, not jewelry, and not medical braces for teeth. Picture what a programmer or typographer would recognize instantly.

Hint 4 Purple Category Clue

Think mid-century award-show trivia: four song titles from the very first Grammy ceremony (1959), not temperatures, witchcraft as a hobby, or Italian greetings in isolation.

Today’s Connections Analysis

Yellow band Dismissive “dream on” phrases

How to identify it: Read each clue as something you might say to reject an impossible request. All four should sound like polite or sarcastic “no.”

Possible confusion: IMPOSSIBLE can feel like a purple wordplay trap—here it is literal. Do not pair it with sensible synonyms or typographic marks.

Green band Synonyms for “reasonable”

How to identify it: Say “That idea is ___” for each word. If the sense is “sensible / well-founded,” you have the green cluster.

Possible confusion: RIGHT also means correct direction—ignore that reading. SOUND is not about audio alone here.

Blue band Keyboard / code symbols

How to identify it: Picture a cheat sheet of characters above the number row or in regex—curly pair, caret, vertical bar, wavy mark.

Possible confusion: PIPE tempts a plumbing group with yellow’s IMPOSSIBLE nowhere near it—here it is the vertical bar symbol, not a tube.

Purple band First Grammy Song of the Year nominees

How to identify it: Treat each clue as a classic song title from 1959 award history—not spells, temperatures, or Italian film titles in isolation.

Possible confusion: WITCHCRAFT and FEVER look thematic; only submit when all four are song names from that specific nominees list.

How to Solve Today’s Puzzle Step by Step

  1. Scan for sarcastic “no chance” language and lock that yellow quartet first—it clears four tiles and separates IMPOSSIBLE from sensible synonyms.

Common Mistakes in Today’s Puzzle

  • Pairing CLEAR with typographic symbols because it can mean “transparent”—green wants the “sensible” sense only.
  • Building a plumbing group around PIPE while BRACE and CARET are still on the board.
  • Submitting IMPOSSIBLE with sensible synonyms because both feel like “judgment words”—they belong to different bands.
  • Forcing FEVER or WITCHCRAFT into a magic/illness theme instead of the song-title list.

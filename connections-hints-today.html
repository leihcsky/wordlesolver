Connections Hints Today (Spoiler-Free)

Hint 1 Easiest group (yellow band)

Four labels you would twist on a range while cooking—heat level and whether the burner is on at all.

Watch out: MEDIUM also describes size or spice elsewhere; here it is the dial between low and high.

Hint 2 Second group (green band)

Synonyms for how strong or powerful something is—think punch, oomph, or “with great ___.”

Do not grab stove HIGH or music KEY; those belong on other bands today.

Hint 3 Third group (blue band)

Terms from a music-theory class—how pitches relate, not how loud the stove is.

Pop-culture memory beats dictionary definitions; SCALE here is not a bathroom device or a fish.

Hint 4 Hardest group (purple band)

Each clue becomes a famous film title when you add the same second word—one is a holiday rodent, one is July 4th.

Meaning alone will not help; this is the “complete the movie name” group saved for last.

How to use these hints

Open hints in color order (yellow → purple). Each card explains the type of link—not the official category headline you see after a correct solve.

  • Yellow band: Solve first—burner dial positions. If a word feels like music or gym intensity, it is probably using the wrong definition.
  • Green band: Hunt strength synonyms; do not pair FORCE with stove HIGH or movie titles.
  • Blue band: Needs theory-class memory; cross off anything that is a kitchen setting or a holiday film fragment.
  • Purple band: Only after three groups are gone—whisper “___ Day” after each clue and listen for blockbuster names.

Today’s Connections Analysis

Why each band behaves the way it does—still spoiler-free on category names. Use this after the expandable hints to confirm you are not one homograph away from a mistake.

Yellow band Stovetop dial labels

How to identify it: HIGH, MEDIUM, OFF, and SIMMER are the four positions you twist while cooking—nothing here is about music keys or physical strength.

Possible confusion: HIGH and MEDIUM tempt an “intensity” mash-up with the green band; OFF also means “not working” in general English—here it is the knob position.

Green band Strength / potency

How to identify it: CONCENTRATION, FORCE, INTENSITY, and MIGHT all describe how powerful something is—test “with great ___” or “sheer ___.”

Possible confusion: CONCENTRATION also means focus or a chemistry lab measure; today it is the “strength” sense only.

Blue band Music-theory terms

How to identify it: INTERVAL, KEY, MODE, and SCALE are textbook harmony vocabulary—think piano lessons, not kitchen knobs.

Possible confusion: KEY unlocks doors; SCALE measures weight—those everyday senses are traps on #1074.

Purple band “___ Day” film titles

How to identify it: GROUNDHOG, INDEPENDENCE, THE LONGEST, and TRAINING each complete a famous movie when you add the same second word.

Possible confusion: INDEPENDENCE alone is a concept, not the link—only the film-title phrase counts.

How to Solve Today’s Puzzle Step by Step

  1. Scan for homographs. List alternate meanings for HIGH, MEDIUM, KEY, SCALE, FORCE, and OFF before you submit—today’s traps are definition switches, not obscure vocabulary.
  2. Lock the yellow band. Once HIGH, MEDIUM, OFF, and SIMMER are off the board, twelve words remain and three mistakes still hurt.
  3. Test the green quartet as strength words. If all four can complete “great ___” or “sheer ___,” submit; if one only works on a stove, stop.
  4. Separate theory from knobs. With green gone, the remaining long words should cluster as music-class terms; KEY is not a door object here.
  5. Finish with “___ Day.” For the last four, say each clue plus the same second word and listen for film franchises.

Common Mistakes in Today’s Puzzle

  • Intensity mash-up: Grouping HIGH, MEDIUM, FORCE, and INTENSITY because they all sound “strong”—only one band uses that idea.
  • KEY confusion: Pulling KEY into a door-lock group while SCALE and MODE sit unused in blue.
  • Movie vs. music: Treating GROUNDHOG as an animal clue instead of half of a film title with the same suffix as TRAINING.
  • OFF everywhere: Using OFF as “cancelled” rather than a burner setting that pairs with SIMMER.
  • One-away panic: Submitting a “close enough” foursome when one word still needs a different rule—NYT charges a mistake for that on #1074.

Today’s Difficulty & Pattern

Overall: medium–hard. Yellow is a clean kitchen solve; green is medium because CONCENTRATION has multiple senses; blue is hard if you never took music theory; purple is hard until you say “___ Day” aloud.

Pattern note: #1074 is a homograph festival—success depends on locking one sense per word before you burn guesses on cross-band overlaps (dial vs. strength vs. theory vs. cinema).

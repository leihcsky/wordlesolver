Connections Hints Today (Spoiler-Free)

Expand each band in difficulty order. Hints describe how to think—official category names stay in the color bars and answer reveal below.

Hint 1 Easiest band (yellow)

Four things you say when you ping someone after a meeting—professional outreach, not airport queues alone.

Why this helps: Yellow is often office-English phrases; if a clue only fits travel or shopping, it probably belongs elsewhere.

Hint 2 Second band (green)

Words for how society expects things to be done—written or not, formal or informal rules of behavior.

Why this helps: Not a political gathering or bespoke tailoring—think etiquette and tradition.

Hint 3 Third band (blue)

Places where stuff rides past you on a moving belt—factory, airport, grocery, or a rotating food lane.

Why this helps: Picture luggage carousels and checkout belts, not email threads or weather patterns.

Hint 4 Hardest band (purple)

Each clue’s opening syllable sounds like a common first name—say the first word aloud and listen for Gary, Lucy, Leo, or Taylor.

Why this helps: Dictionary definitions will mislead; this band is sound-alike wordplay, not literal categories.

How to use these hints

  • Yellow first: Lock the workplace outreach phrases before multi-word clues split across bands.
  • Green next: Test etiquette vocabulary—if one clue is only a political event, stop.
  • Blue when stuck: Think physical belts and carousels, not “lines” of people or code.
  • Purple last: Whisper the first syllable of each clue like a name before you submit.

Today’s Connections Analysis

How to think about each difficulty band—without naming answers or official category titles. Use after the expandable hints; reveal groups below when you want the full solution.

Yellow band Workplace outreach phrases

How to identify it: Four multi-word expressions you would use when re-establishing contact—follow-ups and quick pings, not single-word nouns.

Possible confusion: Do not pair travel check-ins with grocery checkout—those need a belt-driven rule.

Green band Social rules & norms

How to identify it: Each clue names an expected way people behave—tradition, habit, or unspoken agreement, not machinery.

Possible confusion: “Convention” as a conference is the trap; here it is custom and norm.

Blue band Conveyor-belt settings

How to identify it: Real-world spots where items move on a belt—manufacturing, baggage, supermarket lanes, or sushi on rails.

Possible confusion: Anything that is only a “line” of people without a physical belt belongs elsewhere.

Purple band Name-sound homophones

How to identify it: The lead of each clue homophones a first name—weather, luggage, slang, and bespoke tailoring all start with name-like sounds.

Possible confusion: Grouping by travel, weather, or fashion themes misses the audio pun entirely.

How to Solve Today’s Puzzle Step by Step

  1. Sort multi-word clues. Yellow and blue both use phrases—decide whether a clue is outreach language or a physical belt venue before you submit.
  2. Lock yellow outreach. Once the four ping-style phrases are gone, twelve words remain and mistakes still hurt.
  3. Separate norms from events. Green is etiquette vocabulary; do not pull conference or factory words into that band.
  4. Picture belts. Blue needs a moving surface—airport carousels and store lanes count; email threads do not.
  5. Finish with name sounds. For purple, say the first chunk of each clue like you are calling a friend’s name.

Common Mistakes in Today’s Puzzle

  • Airport only: Treating check-in as travel while the yellow band is office correspondence.
  • Conference trap: Putting convention with political events instead of social custom.
  • Line vs. belt: Grouping checkout with queue lines that are not conveyor-driven.
  • Weather theme: Pulling El Niño into a climate group instead of the name-sound purple set.
  • One-away panic: Submitting three outreach phrases plus a social norm—one clue still needs the green rule.

Today’s Difficulty & Pattern

Overall: medium–hard. Yellow outreach is clean; green needs careful sense-checking; blue is visual trivia; purple is hard until you try the name homophone trick aloud.

Pattern note: #1076 splits phrases (yellow) from places (blue) and sounds (purple)—homographs on “check,” “convention,” and “custom” burn guesses fast.

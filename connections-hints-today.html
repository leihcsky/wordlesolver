Yellow band Art paint types

How to identify it: Four painting media—fast-drying plastic paint, opaque watercolor cousin, classic linseed medium, and egg-tempera tradition.

Possible confusion: OIL also means cooking fat; here it must mean the artist’s bottle on an easel.

Green band Flair and spirit words

How to identify it: Synonyms for verve and style—Italian gusto, French panache, lively verve, plus a French-loanword pantry item that shares the “spirit” etymology angle.

Possible confusion: VINEGAR tempts a condiment band with SALT; test the flair frame on all four before you submit.

Blue band Rap group name openers

How to identify it: First words of iconic groups—Beastie ___, Public ___, Run ___, Salt ___ (the last pairs with a pepper in the group’s full name).

Possible confusion: RUN also means jogging; PUBLIC also means audience—complete the hip-hop names to validate.

Purple band Ghost compounds

How to identify it: Same leading word plus each clue forms a familiar compound—haunted kitchen, fiery chili, abandoned settlement, uncredited author.

Possible confusion: PEPPER also belongs in cooking sets; here it must complete the spooky phrase, not a spice rack.

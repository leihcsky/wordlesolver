    IT support or hotel assistance — read it as the first half of a two-word workplace station, not a verb asking for aid alone.

    Airport or hotel registration counter — pairs with Help as front-desk phrases, not wellness check-ins or calendar events.

    Adjustable workstation for working upright — confirms the suffix is desk, not posture exercises or standing ovations.

How these clues fit together

Help and Check-in sound like workplace tasks until Rolltop forces a furniture read. News fits a newsroom station, and Standing closes the ergonomic desk angle — each clue is a specific kind of desk, not generic office supplies. Wrong rabbit holes include "workplace services" or "ergonomic tools" without the shared desk suffix.

Complete Wordle Helper Guide: How to Use Our Solver Tool

Master our free Wordle helper tool with this step-by-step guide and solve any puzzle in minutes

Welcome to the ultimate Wordle helper guide! Whether you're stuck on today's puzzle or want to improve your Wordle-solving skills, our free AI-powered tool is here to help. This comprehensive tutorial will walk you through every step of using our Wordle solver, from entering your first guess to finding the winning word.

What is a Wordle Helper?

A Wordle helper (also known as a Wordle solver or Wordle assistant) is an intelligent tool that analyzes your guesses and provides optimal word suggestions based on the feedback you receive from the game. Our tool uses a sophisticated algorithm and a database of over 3,000 valid 5-letter words to give you the best possible recommendations.

Unlike random word generators, our Wordle helper understands the game's color-coding system and applies smart filtering to eliminate impossible words, saving you time and improving your success rate.

Step-by-Step Guide: Using Our Wordle Helper

1

Play Your First Guess in Wordle

Start by playing the official Wordle game on the New York Times website. Enter your first 5-letter word guess. We recommend starting with vowel-rich words like:

  • ADIEU - Contains 4 vowels (A, I, E, U)
  • AUDIO - Great vowel coverage with common consonants
  • SLATE - Balanced mix of common letters
  • CRANE - Popular starting word among Wordle experts
2

Enter Your Guess in Our Tool

Open our Wordle Solver Tool in a new tab. You'll see a grid similar to the Wordle game. Type your first guess using either:

  • Your keyboard - Simply type the letters
  • Virtual keyboard - Click the on-screen letter buttons

The letters will appear in the first row of tiles. Press ENTER when you've completed the 5-letter word.

3

Set the Color Feedback for Each Letter

This is the most important step! After entering your word, you need to tell our tool what feedback Wordle gave you. Click each letter tile to cycle through the three color states:

A

Green (Correct Position)

The letter is in the word AND in the correct position. This letter is locked in!

E

Yellow (Wrong Position)

The letter is in the word BUT in the wrong position. It needs to move to a different spot.

X

Gray (Not in Word)

The letter is NOT in the word at all. Our tool will exclude all words containing this letter.

⚠️ Important:

ALL tiles must be colored before you can get suggestions. White/empty tiles mean the color hasn't been set yet. Make sure every letter in every row has a color (green, yellow, or gray).

4

Click "Get Suggestions"

Once all your letters are properly colored, click the big green "Get Suggestions" button. Our AI-powered algorithm will instantly analyze your input and display a ranked list of the best possible words.

The suggestions are sorted by probability, with the most likely words at the top. Each suggestion is a valid English word that matches all the constraints from your colored feedback.

5

Try the Top Suggestion in Wordle

Go back to your Wordle game and enter the top word from our suggestions list. This word has the highest probability of being correct based on your previous guesses.

If it's not the answer, don't worry! You'll get new color feedback that will help narrow down the possibilities even further.

6

Repeat Until You Solve It!

Continue the process: enter each new guess in our tool, set the colors based on Wordle's feedback, and get fresh suggestions. With each guess, the list of possible words gets smaller and more accurate.

Most puzzles can be solved in 3-4 guesses using our helper tool. You'll maintain your winning streak and improve your average score!

Pro Tips for Using Our Wordle Helper

  • Start with vowel-rich words to quickly identify which vowels are in the puzzle
  • Double-check your colors before clicking "Get Suggestions" - accuracy is key!
  • Use the "Load Example" button to see how the tool works with a sample puzzle
  • Consider multiple suggestions - sometimes the second or third option might be the answer
  • Use "Reset" to start fresh if you make a mistake or want to try a different approach

Common Questions

Q: Is this tool free to use?

A: Yes! Our Wordle helper is 100% free with no registration required. Use it as many times as you want.

Q: Does it work on mobile devices?

A: Absolutely! Our tool is fully responsive and works perfectly on smartphones and tablets.

Q: What if I get an error message?

A: Make sure all tiles in your entered rows are colored (green, yellow, or gray). White tiles indicate unset colors and will prevent the tool from working.

Does Wordle Repeat Words?

2026 UPDATE: YES, IT DOES!

March 04, 2026 • 5 min read

For years, the golden rule of Wordle was simple: "The answer has never been used before." Players meticulously tracked past solutions to rule out thousands of words.

That rule is now broken. As of February 2026, the New York Times has officially updated its policy, and we've already seen the first repeated word in Wordle history. Here is everything you need to know about this major change.

The Official Policy Change

🚨 Update: February 2, 2026

The New York Times Games team announced via their Gameplay newsletter that previously used words are now back in play. This marks the end of the "no repeats" era that lasted from 2021 to early 2026.

According to the announcement, the goal is to allow the editors to mix repeated words in more randomly, rather than waiting until the entire original word list is exhausted. This change ensures that the game remains unpredictable and allows the editorial team to create "magical, serendipitous moments" by tying answers to current events or cultural moments, even if the word was used years ago.

The First Repeated Word: A "Groundhog Day" Moment

On February 2, 2026 (Groundhog Day), Wordle #1628's answer was:

CIGAR

Previous Appearance: June 19, 2021 (Wordle #1)

The choice of CIGAR was a brilliant editorial wink. Not only is it a reference to the movie Groundhog Day (where days repeat), but CIGAR was also the very first answer in Wordle history (Wordle #1).

By reusing the first-ever solution on a holiday famous for repetition, the NYT signaled clearly that the old rules no longer apply.

How This Changes Your Strategy

If you're a serious player who uses "past answer lists" to eliminate potential guesses, you need to update your strategy immediately.

  • 1

    Don't Rule Out Past Answers

    Just because a word like CRANE or SLATE has been used before doesn't mean it won't appear again tomorrow.

  • 2

    Focus on Probability, Not History

    Our Wordle Solver still works perfectly because it focuses on letter probability and pattern matching, which are timeless strategies.

  • 3

    Expect the Unexpected

    The editors now have the freedom to choose any 5-letter word that fits the vibe of the day, regardless of its history.

Summary

Wordle has evolved. While the core gameplay remains the same, the finite list of ~2,300 unique solutions is no longer a constraint. Whether you love the added unpredictability or miss the ability to cross off words, one thing is certain: Wordle is here to stay, and no word is off-limits anymore.

What This Frequency Chart Shows & Why It Helps

Letter frequency analysis reveals which letters appear most often in Wordle's valid word list. This data helps you:

  • Choose better starter words: Prioritize words with high-frequency letters
  • Eliminate possibilities faster: Know which letters are less likely to appear
  • Make informed guesses: Understand letter distribution by position
  • Improve win rate: Use data-driven strategy instead of guessing randomly

The data is based on analysis of Wordle's official word list (2,315 valid 5-letter words) and represents actual letter frequencies in the game.

Least Common Letters & Why They Appear Less

üîç Rarest Letters in Wordle:

  • J (2 occurrences): Very few common 5-letter words contain J
  • Q (3 occurrences): Q almost always requires U, limiting word options
  • Z (5 occurrences): Uncommon in English; mostly in words like FROZE, PRIZE
  • X (7 occurrences): Rare; appears in words like BOXER, MIXER, PIXEL
  • K (44 occurrences): More common than X/Z/Q but still relatively rare

These rare letters should generally be avoided in early guesses unless you have specific information suggesting they're in the answer. Focus on high-frequency letters first to maximize information gain.

How to Use Frequency Data When Guessing

Starter Words Based on Frequency

üéØ Top Frequency-Based Starters:

  • STARE - S, T, A, R, E (all top 6 letters)
  • SLATE - S, L, A, T, E (covers high-frequency letters)
  • AROSE - A, R, O, S, E (excellent frequency distribution)
  • ROAST - R, O, A, S, T (strong position coverage)
  • IRATE - I, R, A, T, E (tests multiple high-frequency letters)

Midgame vs Endgame Uses

üìä Midgame (Guesses 2-3):

Use frequency data to test remaining high-frequency letters. If you've already tested E, A, R, focus on O, I, T, N, S. This systematic approach eliminates possibilities efficiently.

üéØ Endgame (Guesses 4-6):

By now, you know which letters are in the word. Use frequency data to predict letter positions. For example, if you have _O_E_, E is most likely in position 4 or 5, and O is likely in position 2.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is E the most common letter?

E is the most frequently used letter in English. It appears in common words like THESE, THERE, WHERE, and many others. This pattern holds true in Wordle's word list.

Should I always use high-frequency letters?

Not always. While high-frequency letters are good for early guesses, you should also consider letter position. For example, if you know the answer ends in -ED, prioritize that pattern over random high-frequency letters.

How does frequency data improve my win rate?

By focusing on high-frequency letters and positions, you eliminate more possibilities with each guess. This reduces the average number of guesses needed to solve the puzzle.

Can I use this data with your Wordle Solver?

Yes! Our Wordle Solver uses frequency analysis combined with entropy calculations to suggest optimal guesses.

Does frequency change with different word lists?

Yes. Different word lists (like Scrabble dictionaries) have different frequency distributions. This data is specific to Wordle's official word list.
